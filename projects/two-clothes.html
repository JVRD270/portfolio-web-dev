The Two Clothes Shop Idea

Clothing Ecommerce brands are emerging in a scale we have never seen before, and this market gets more competitive by the second. For anyone in it, it is important to distinct themselves and get their brands out there. At the same time, a unique look, ideas and designs are not easy to come by. The Two Clothes Shop is a unique demo store, focused on displaying what good design and a clear goal can do for a business. The user experience is enhanced to make sure anyone can follow along easily and find what they are looking for, while at the same time connect to the colors and imagery.

Challenges

Challenges in Web Development usually come in the form of concepts and structure, while putting everything together is the easy part. With that said, building a new brand together with a store always comes with doubts on what to display, what kind of features can captivate the desired user and how to make sure they can find what they are looking for. It becomes easier when using an outstanding theme as Prestige, which already brings several useful features and a great user interface to begin with.

When it comes to the technical part, making sure a website is responsive is of utmost importance, and doing it with excellence takes years of practice. Of course, Shopify and Prestige helped a lot in this project, considering how they are structured and adaptable. When going into code, though, I had to be very careful to not disrupt anything and incorporate new features seamlessly. Liquid language alongside JavaScript are always great tools to work with.

Unique Design

The design approach was very simple, as I always like to do. Using the qualities of the Prestige theme, the idea was to create a lifestyle brand that would resonate with the younger generation. Colors were selected based on that as well, with a mix of hotter and lively with red and pink, and freshness with white and grey.

User Experience

"Good UX is good business."

- Andrew Kucheriavy

As for the experience, making sure anything that is clickable is clearly indicated and has a considerable constrast is important. Mega Menus were added to assist navigation and add an additional touch of imagery, along with my favorite feature, the shop the look block. Our friend STL here is an awesome addition to a website like this, giving 3 clickable options in a picture that will highlight specific products (check images below). With these, we add to the fun and interactivity of the page, and also make sure all steps towards the purchase are clear and easy to follow.

End Result

With a clear message being portrayed here, easy to follow experience for the user, a fully responsive website and a established brand, I am very excited about this project, and fully confident anyone, including me, would love to have their store looking like this!
